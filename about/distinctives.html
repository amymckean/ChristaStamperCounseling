What is biblical counseling?

Biblical counseling is whole-person care — soul, body, and relationships — for people created in the image of God. It is grounded in the conviction that God speaks to us through Scripture, which speaks powerfully into every part of human life, including our deepest struggles and suffering. It is not merely quoting Bible verses at a problem. It is the careful, compassionate work of bringing the truth, comfort, and hope of God’s Word into the real details of a person’s story.

Christa’s approach follows the counseling distinctives taught at Westminster Theological Seminary, where she completed her Master of Arts in Counseling, and by the Christian Counseling & Educational Foundation (CCEF), where she completed her Formation Certificate and Essentials of Biblical Counseling training.

A few convictions that shape Christa’s counseling

God draws near to the suffering

We have a suffering Savior who identifies with our pain. Scripture shows us a God who is not distant from suffering but present with His people in it. Biblical counseling is a ministry of walking alongside the brokenhearted in the same way our compassionate God draws near to us.

“The Lord is near to the brokenhearted and saves the crushed in spirit.”

— Psalm 34:18 (ESV)

“For we do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but one who in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin.”

— Hebrews 4:15 (ESV)

People are more than their symptoms

We are complex beings shaped by our bodies, histories, relationships, circumstances, and — most importantly — by what we love, fear, and worship. Effective care listens to the whole person, not just the presenting problem.

“Keep your heart with all vigilance, for from it flow the springs of life.”

— Proverbs 4:23 (ESV)

“You formed my inward parts; you knitted me together in my mother’s womb. I praise you, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made.”

— Psalm 139:13–14 (ESV)

Change doesn’t follow a script

Every person’s story is unique. For one person, a particular passage of Scripture may bring breakthrough. For another, it may be the simple gift of being truly heard and understood. For many, lasting change comes through slow, ordinary faithfulness over time. Christa listens carefully to discern what each person needs in their season.

“Now the Lord is the Spirit, and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is freedom. And we all, with unveiled face, beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another. For this comes from the Lord who is the Spirit.”

— 2 Corinthians 3:17–18 (ESV)

“Do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewal of your mind, that by testing you may discern what is the will of God, what is good and acceptable and perfect.”

— Romans 12:2 (ESV)

Change in light of God’s redemptive story

Christa helps people understand their lives in light of the grand redemptive history of Scripture — creation, fall, redemption, and glorification. This bigger story brings hope, meaning, and direction even in the midst of suffering.

“And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good, for those who are called according to his purpose.”

— Romans 8:28 (ESV)

“Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come.”

— 2 Corinthians 5:17 (ESV)

Biblical counseling is distinct from licensed clinical therapy. Christa is a trained biblical counselor, not a licensed mental health professional, and her sessions are not a substitute for licensed treatment when that is needed.
